Frequencies Of The Forgotten

Tango Byte • 2025 • Unity

Frequencies of the Forgotten is a first-person psychological horror game built around one core idea: what if the horror game was actively listening?

Frequencies of the Forgotten is a first-person psychological horror game where your own microphone becomes the main input—and the main threat. You play as Alex, an audio engineer called back to a defunct 1980s radio station after a ghostly late-night transmission from his long-missing brother. Trapped in a decaying broadcast facility built over a secret research complex, you’ll navigate by sound, solve audio-driven puzzles, and speak directly into the darkness as a signal-bound Entity hunts you through the static. With no weapons, only your tools and your voice, Frequencies of the Forgotten is a slow-burn, non-combat horror experience where the game quite literally listens to you...

Key Features

  • Mic-Driven Horror: The game listens through your real microphone—your speech, noise, and silence all affect gameplay.
  • Non-Combat Survival: Explore, hide, reroute power, and stay quiet. There are no weapons.
  • Audio-Focused Puzzles: Tune frequencies, decode spectrograms, and operate analog broadcast equipment.
  • Sanity Effects: As fear rises, objects shift, rooms rearrange, and familiar paths become unstable.
  • Analog 1980s Broadcast Aesthetic: A grounded, atmospheric radio station built over a hidden research complex.
  • Streamer-Friendly: The world reacts to your voice and chat reactions, making every playthrough unique.
The Game That Listens

Frequencies of the Forgotten features a dynamic audio recognition system that uses your real microphone. Whisper commands to open sealed doors, call out into the darkness at your own risk, or hold your breath as the Entity drifts by. Every sound you make can help you progress—or expose you.
